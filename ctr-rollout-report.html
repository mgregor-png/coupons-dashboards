New Platform Rollout — US CTR Performance

Phase 1: 48 merchants (Mar 18) · Phase 2: 3,500+ merchants (Apr 1, planned) · Data: Tableau · Updated March 27

Measurement Plan — DMMM Framework
Goal
Validate new platform doesn't degrade CTR before scaling to 100% of US merchants
Tactics
Phased rollout: 4 → 54 → 3,500+ merchants. 50/50 experiments on top 30 revenue merchants. Legacy protection for 407 high-value merchants.
KPIs
CTR (primary), CWV (LCP, INP, CLS), bounce rate, error rates, SEO rankings
Targets
CTR neutral or positive vs legacy baseline (same merchant, matched period). CWV: maintain "Passed" status.
Segments
(1) Code Position 1: has P1 code vs no P1 code
(2) Content Quality: quality offers vs spam/filler
(3) Traffic Allocation: 100% / 50-50 / legacy
(4) Revenue Tier

Framework: Avinash Kaushik's Digital Marketing & Measurement Model (DMMM)

What We're Measuring

Platform Performance Test (50/50 Split)

Top 30 revenue merchants run 50/50 legacy vs new platform. This is the controlled experiment — same merchants, same time, same offers. Controls for seasonality and offer quality.
This is the definitive signal for platform impact on CTR.

SEO Test (Whole-Page Rollout)

48 merchants on 100% new platform. Required for SEO measurement — search engines need to see the full new page. Before/after comparison is confounded by offer quality changes and seasonality.
Aggregate (mass) signal is usable. Individual merchant WoW is not reliable without context.
