Rollout Strategy

We're migrating US coupon merchant pages from legacy to a new platform in controlled phases, validating performance at each stage before increasing exposure.

POC
Jan - Mar 14
4 merchants on 100% new platform. Validated core functionality, page rendering, and caching. Confirmed no critical issues - platform ready for broader testing.
Phase 1
Mar 18
48 → 54 merchants - started with 48 (38 at 100% new, 10 at 50/50 split), expanded to 54. First controlled performance measurement. Results: aggregate CTR stable, site speed improvements confirmed. ✓ Completed
Phase 2
Apr 1 - NOW
3,524 merchants - expanded to all US merchants with conservative traffic allocation:
• 407 merchants kept on 100% legacy (high-revenue + SEO-critical protection)
• 41 merchants on 50/50 A/B split (top revenue - controlled experiment)
• ~3,033 merchants receive 33% traffic on new platform, 67% on legacy
• Phase 1 merchants continue unchanged
✓ Deployed - monitoring for CTR, revenue, and error signals.
Phase 3
TBD
Increase traffic allocation - if Phase 2 signals are positive (CTR neutral or better, no revenue impact), increase the 33% split to 66% or 100% for the long-tail merchants. Gradually reduce the legacy-protected bucket. Decision based on 1-2 weeks of Phase 2 data.
Measurement Plan - DMMM Framework
Goal
Validate new platform doesn't degrade CTR before scaling to 100% of US merchants
Tactics
Phased rollout: 4 → 54 → 3,524 merchants. 50/50 experiments on top 30 revenue merchants. Legacy protection for 407 high-value merchants.
KPIs
CTR (primary), CWV (LCP, INP, CLS), bounce rate, error rates, SEO rankings
Targets
CTR neutral or positive vs legacy baseline (same merchant, matched period). CWV: maintain "Passed" status.
Segments
(1) Code Position 1: has P1 code vs no P1 code
(2) Content Quality: quality offers vs spam/filler
(3) Traffic Allocation: 100% / 50-50 / 33-67 / legacy
(4) Revenue Tier

Framework: Avinash Kaushik's Digital Marketing & Measurement Model (DMMM)

What We're Measuring

Platform Performance Test (50/50 Split)

Top 30 revenue merchants run 50/50 legacy vs new platform. This is the controlled experiment - same merchants, same time, same offers. Controls for seasonality and offer quality.
This is the definitive signal for platform impact on CTR and revenue.

Gradual Rollout (33% Traffic Split)

~3,033 remaining merchants receive 33% of traffic on new platform, 67% on legacy. The 407 legacy-protected merchants serve as a control group for aggregate comparison.
Compare aggregate CTR of 33% cohort vs legacy-protected cohort to detect platform-level issues.
Decision criteria for Phase 3: Increase traffic allocation from 33% to 66%+ if after 1-2 weeks: (1) 50/50 A/B shows CTR neutral or better, (2) aggregate long-tail CTR does not drop >10% vs legacy-protected cohort, (3) no revenue per session degradation. Rollback trigger: significant CTR or revenue drop correlated with platform change across multiple cohorts.
