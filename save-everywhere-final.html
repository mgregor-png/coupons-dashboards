Save Everywhere with Groupon

Coupons content provides a mechanism for Groupon to be part of our customers' shopping experience across thousands of brands and millions of checkouts.

Author: Martin Gregor Date: April 2026 Goal: Extend Groupon's reach beyond the core app
By delivering coupons content through a Safari extension within the Groupon app, we extend Groupon's reach across all their shopping experiences.
This captures users off-platform and through Groupon Bucks brings them back into the app.
Big Insight #1: The highest-frequency savings apps (Fetch, Temu, Shopkick) have decoupled "reason to open" from "intent to transact." Groupon currently requires purchase intent to justify an open. Every idea below creates an open-worthy trigger independent of active deal hunting.
Big Insight #2: Groupon's affiliate coupons content is a hidden frequency asset stuck on the web. People need promo codes constantly - every online purchase is a coupon moment. This content currently lives on groupon.com/coupons (SEO-driven, visit-and-leave). Moving it into the app creates a "save everywhere" utility layer - the bridge between "I open Groupon for a spa deal once a month" and "I open Groupon every time I shop online."

The Cross-Pollination Flywheel

Affiliate coupons and core deals reinforce each other when both live in the app.

User needs a Nike code → Opens Groupon app → Sees daily deal / local offer → Books a spa deal → Earns Bucks in wallet → Comes back for next code

Every coupon lookup is an exposure to a core deal. Every core deal visit is an exposure to coupons. The wallet connects both.

Safari Extension - The App Comes to You

Rather than the Genie extension which only works on desktop, this works on mobile - where our customers shop! 61% of our traffic is mobile.

As an extension of the app, acquisition is supercharged using the existing app base - 8-10M MAU, no separate download needed.

Integrating data from users' off-Groupon shopping experience into their Groupon profile enhances opportunity for personalisation.

A Safari Web Extension bundled inside the Groupon iOS app automatically surfaces relevant coupon codes, cashback offers, and Groupon Bucks opportunities when users shop in mobile Safari. No separate install - it activates when the user enables it in Safari settings.

How it works: User visits nike.com in Safari → extension overlay slides up showing "3 codes available + 4% cashback" → one tap to apply → Bucks earned flow back to Groupon wallet → next app open surfaces personalised local deals based on shopping behaviour.

Why this is huge: Groupon's app currently only captures value when users actively open it. The Safari extension makes Groupon present in every mobile shopping session - turning passive app holders into active daily users.

Competitor proof: Honey (inside PayPal app), Rakuten, and Capital One Shopping all ship Safari extensions inside their iOS apps today. Honey is in crisis (lost 8M users, class action lawsuit) - creating a market window.

Safari Extension + Cashback/Bucks + Coupons in the App - Why We Need All Three

Rather than a standalone extension with just coupons content, the real value is the extension + cashback/Bucks + coupons account/app integration.

1 Safari Extension = Reach

Extends Groupon into every shopping session on mobile Safari. The distribution channel. Works on the 61% of traffic that desktop Genie can't reach.

2 Cashback / Groupon Bucks = Value Creation

Gives us a way of creating valuable content for the user without being reliant on codes issued by merchants. Most importantly, cashback opens the doorway to an upsell into Groupon Bucks - pulling users back to the core app.

3 Coupons in the App = Personalisation

Integration into the account opens the way for more personalisation and messaging. Allow customers to follow their favourite brands for alerts when coupons/cashback offers are available.

A standalone coupons extension is limited. All three together create an ecosystem.

Part B: Affiliate Coupons as an Engagement Layer

Ideas that use Groupon's existing coupons/promo code content to drive app frequency. These complement the Safari extension - they're not separate initiatives.

A1 Follow Stores + Code Alerts
POC: Low effort Recommended Affiliate coupons
What

Let users follow 10-20 favourite online retailers (Nike, Sephora, Amazon, etc.) in the app. Push a notification when a new code appears or an existing code improves for a followed store. "Nike just dropped a 25% off code - tap to grab it."

Why it drives frequency

People shop at the same stores repeatedly. Honey's store-follow alerts and Droplist drive the majority of their re-engagement. Each notification is permission-based (user chose the store) so it feels like a service, not spam. And every alert-driven app open exposes the user to local deals too.

Groupon fit

Groupon has codes for thousands of retailers already. The content exists - it's just not personalized or push-enabled. This is the single lowest-effort, highest-impact move for affiliate content in the app.

POC

Follow button on retailer pages + notification trigger when new code is detected + simple "My Stores" screen in-app. 4-week test with a user cohort.

A2 Weekly "Best Codes" Digest
POC: Low effort Recommended Affiliate coupons
What

Every Monday morning, push a single notification: "This week's top codes: 30% off ASOS, 25% Nike, 20% Uber Eats + 3 more." Links to a curated in-app landing page. Personalised by shopping history / followed stores over time.

Why it drives frequency

Predictable cadence = habit formation. This is Flipp's playbook (weekly grocery flyers → 10M+ downloads, 4.8 stars). Users learn "check Groupon Monday for the week's best codes." The digest format also solves push fatigue - one notification, many codes.

Groupon fit

The content already exists - this is purely curation + push + a landing page. Could be manual initially (editor picks 7 codes), automated later.

A3 Code Expiry Countdown + "Use It or Lose It" Alerts
POC: Low effort Affiliate coupons
What

When a user saves or views a code, track it. If the code is expiring within 24 hours, push an alert: "Your 30% off ASOS code expires tonight." Prominent expiry timers on saved codes in-app.

Why it drives frequency

Urgency + loss aversion. A code you saved that's about to expire is nearly impossible to ignore. This is the same FOMO mechanic as flash sales, but applied to something the user already expressed interest in. High-intent, high-conversion notifications.

Groupon fit

Codes already have expiry dates in Groupon's system. This is mostly a notification trigger + UI treatment. Very low effort, very high engagement potential.

A4 Web-to-App Bridge: "Save This Code to the App"
POC: Low-Med effort Affiliate coupons
What

On groupon.com/coupons pages (SEO traffic), add a CTA: "Save this code to the Groupon app" that deep-links into the app with the code pre-loaded. Converts one-time web visitors into app users. Once in the app, they're in the ecosystem.

Why it drives frequency

Groupon's coupons pages get millions of SEO visits. Currently these users arrive, grab a code, and leave. This is the bridge - convert web traffic into app installs/opens. Each converted user enters the flywheel (sees local deals, earns Bucks, gets push alerts).

Groupon fit

Uses existing traffic that Groupon already owns. No new content creation. The deep-link technology exists (branch.io, etc.). This is the funnel that feeds all other engagement ideas.

A5 Post-Use Bucks: "Did This Code Work?"
POC: Medium effort Affiliate coupons
What

After a user copies a code, prompt them to confirm: "Did this code work? Yes → earn 10 Bucks. No → we'll find you another." This creates a closing loop + code verification data + Bucks flowing into the wallet.

Why it drives frequency

Turns a one-way interaction (grab code, leave) into a round-trip (grab code, come back, confirm, earn Bucks). The Bucks reward makes the return visit worthwhile. The verification data improves code quality for everyone - solving the #1 frustration with coupon sites (broken codes).

Groupon fit

Solves a real product problem (code quality) while driving engagement. The Bucks feed the Savings Wallet.

A6 "Save Everywhere" - Unified Savings Dashboard
POC: Medium effort Core + Affiliate
What

A single screen showing all your savings opportunities in one place: local deals near you + online codes for your favourite stores + available Bucks + active watches. "Your personalised savings hub." Think Rakuten dashboard but combining local + online.

Why it drives frequency

Positions Groupon as "the one app for all savings" - not just local deals, not just coupons. The combination is the moat: nobody else has both local deals AND online codes in the same app. Users open to see "what can I save on today?" across all categories.

Groupon fit

Groupon literally has both content types - local deals (core) and online codes (affiliate). They just live in separate silos today. Merging them into a unified experience is Groupon's unique positioning that Honey, Rakuten, or RetailMeNot can't replicate.

Recommendation: The "Save Everywhere" Bundle

Layers that turn Groupon from "deal marketplace I visit monthly" into "savings companion I open daily." The affiliate ideas are the frequency engine; the Safari extension is the reach engine; Bucks are the retention engine.

1. Safari Extension - the always-on presence Bundled inside the iOS app, surfaces codes and cashback in every mobile Safari shopping session. 8-10M MAU base = instant distribution. No separate download.
2. Follow Stores + Code Alerts (A1) - the everyday utility Users follow their favourite online stores. Alerts drive app opens for every shopping moment, not just local deals. This is the bridge between monthly and daily engagement.
3. Weekly Best Codes Digest (A2) - Monday morning ritual Curated weekly push with the best codes. Predictable cadence builds habit.

Foundation: Groupon Bucks wallet connects everything. Bucks earned from the Safari extension, code alerts, and post-use verification are spent on core local deals. This is the flywheel.

Risks to Name Upfront

  • Push notification fatigue: Multiple ideas rely on push. Solution: one "Morning Brief" digest aggregating all signals, not 5 separate pushes. Store alerts can fire individually (permission-based).
  • Affiliate attribution integrity: Honey's cookie-overwriting scandal is a cautionary tale. Groupon must use clean, transparent attribution. Never overwrite another affiliate's cookies.
  • Coupons content quality: Broken codes are the #1 frustration. Post-Use Bucks (A5) and community voting help, but code quality needs to be high enough to earn trust.
  • Safari extension App Store risk: Apple controls the platform. Extension must comply with App Store guidelines - no background tracking, transparent permissions, clear user value.
  • iOS engineering capacity: Safari extension requires iOS-specific expertise. The 2-week spike is designed to validate feasibility before committing further.
