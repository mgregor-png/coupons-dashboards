Strategy Recommendation

Save Everywhere

Coupons content provides a mechanism for Groupon to be part of our customers' shopping experience across thousands of brands and millions of checkouts.

For: Adam, Dusan & Rebecca  ·  April 2026
The Premise

Groupon only gets opened when someone wants to buy something.

The highest-frequency savings apps have decoupled "reason to open" from "intent to transact." Groupon hasn't.

Why this matters

Fetch, Temu, and Shopkick users open daily because there's always something to check, earn, or discover - whether or not they're buying. Groupon sits on the other end: high-intent, low-frequency. Monthly visits instead of daily opens.

The result: monetisation depends entirely on conversion rather than engagement. To double revenue per UDV, we need to multiply the number of times people open the app - not just optimise what happens when they do.

Hidden asset: Groupon's affiliate coupons content is a frequency engine stuck on the web. People need promo codes constantly. Moving this content into the app creates a "save everywhere" utility layer - the bridge between monthly and daily.
By delivering coupons content through a Safari extension within the Groupon app, we can extend Groupon's reach to deliver value for our customers across all their shopping experiences.
Foundation

Groupon Bucks - The Connective Tissue

Every idea connects through one mechanism - a visible, growing balance that gives users a reason to come back even when they're not shopping.

User needs a promo code → Opens Groupon app → Sees a local deal → Books it → Earns Groupon Bucks → Comes back for next code
How Groupon Bucks ties it all together

Bucks accumulate from streaks, coupon use, code verifications, and referrals. They're spent on core deals. This creates a shared currency between the affiliate coupon world and the core deals world - without it, the two sides don't reinforce each other.

"You've earned $14.20 this month" - users check their balance even when they're not shopping. That's the habit loop.

How It Works

Five core mechanics that make Save Everywhere a viable strategy for Groupon.

1

A Safari extension is added to the Groupon app. When a user downloads the app and enables the extension, Groupon will pop up when they shop at leading retailers on their phone.

2

Coupons content is strengthened by cashback offers which upsell into Groupon Bucks - leading the user back to Groupon.

3

Rather than the Genie extension which only works on desktop, this works on mobile - where our customers shop!

4

As an extension of the app, acquisition is supercharged using the existing app base.

5

Integrating data from users' off-Groupon shopping experience into their Groupon profile enhances opportunity for personalisation.

Technical detail

How the extension works under the hood:

  • Content script injected into Safari pages detects merchant domains (matching against Groupon's coupon database)
  • On checkout/cart pages, it injects a floating banner at the bottom of the viewport - non-intrusive, dismissible
  • Tapping "Apply" pastes the code into the promo field on the page (same approach as Honey)
  • "Claim Bucks" button uses a universal link to deep-link into the Groupon app with the savings amount pre-loaded
  • The app lands on a success screen showing Bucks earned + a "Follow this store" prompt + local deals

One-time setup (two steps): After installing the Groupon app, user enables the extension in Settings → Safari → Extensions, then sets Website Access to "All Websites." Two taps, done forever. The app can prompt and deep-link to these settings.

Key constraints:

  • Safari only - doesn't work in Chrome, Firefox, or in-app browsers (~55% of iOS mobile browsing uses Safari)
  • Content Security Policy - some sites with strict CSP may limit injected UI; the extension should gracefully fall back to Safari's native popup sheet
  • App Store guidelines - Section 4.4 says extensions can't include "marketing or advertising," but Honey and Rakuten are approved, so coupon-finding is accepted
Frequency Engine

Make the app useful every time someone shops online.

These ideas use content Groupon already has to drive habitual app engagement.

Follow Stores + Code Alerts

Follow your favourite retailers. Get a push when a new code drops: "Nike just posted 25% off - tap to grab it." Permission-based, so it feels like a service. Every alert opens the app - and exposes the user to local deals.

Detail

Honey's store-follow alerts drive the majority of their re-engagement. Groupon has codes for thousands of retailers - the content exists, it's just not personalised or push-enabled. This is the single lowest-effort, highest-impact move for affiliate content in the app.

POC: Follow button on retailer pages + push trigger on new code detection + "My Stores" screen. 4-week cohort test.

Weekly "Best Codes" Digest

Every Monday: "This week's top codes: 30% off ASOS, 25% Nike, 20% Uber Eats + 3 more." One notification, one curated landing page. Predictable cadence = habit formation.

Detail

Flipp built a $100M+ business on weekly grocery flyers alone (10M+ downloads). The principle: users learn "check Groupon Monday for the week's best codes." Could be manual initially (editor picks 7 codes), automated later. Pairs with the Friday Weekend Content for a two-beat weekly rhythm.

Unknowns

What We Need to Validate

The 2-week iOS engineering spike answers the biggest question. The rest are measurable through testing.

  • ?
    iOS App Store: Can we bundle a Safari extension inside the Groupon app? Honey (PayPal) and Rakuten do this today, but Groupon's app structure may differ. The 2-week spike validates this before any build commitment.
  • ?
    Extension activation rate: Estimated 10-30% range. Honey achieves ~30% activation from the PayPal app. 10% is our conservative floor. Actual rate determines whether this is a $4M or $16M opportunity.
  • ?
    iOS share of app users: Estimated ~55% based on general market data. Need confirmed numbers from Radomir/analytics to refine the addressable base.
  • ?
    Monthly app install/update rates: New installs get the extension automatically. Existing users need an app update. Update velocity affects ramp timeline.
  • ?
    App Store submission process: Review timeline and any guideline changes since Honey/Rakuten were approved. The spike should include a test submission.
The Full Picture

Why We Need All Three

Rather than a standalone extension with just coupons content, we believe the real value of this feature would be based on the extension + cashback/bucks + coupons account/app integration.

🌎

Safari Extension

= Reach

Inserts Groupon into every mobile shopping session. Turns 8-10M app users into extension users with zero acquisition cost.

💰

Cashback / Groupon Bucks

= Value Creation

A growing balance that gives users a reason to come back. Earned off-platform, redeemed on Groupon deals. The pull-back mechanism.

👤

Coupons in the App

= Personalisation

Off-Groupon shopping data flows into user profiles. Enables intent-based recommendations across all of Groupon's inventory.

The extension without Bucks is just a coupon tool ($6/user). Bucks without the extension has no earning mechanism outside the app. Both without account integration means no personalisation. All three together create a flywheel where each piece makes the others more valuable.

Complementary Strategy

How This Complements Personalisation

Viktor fixes the bucket. We fill it with more water.

Viktor: Fix the IN-APP Experience

  • Personalise by L4 affinity (88.5% have a dominant category)
  • Fix the feed, search, checkout (62% never click, 77% abandon)
  • Three pillars: Autopilot, Discovery, Relationship
  • Target: 322K L4 users, $4.8-6.8M upside

Us: Capture OFF-PLATFORM Value

  • Convert anonymous coupons traffic → registered users
  • Follow users into Safari/Chrome via extensions
  • Bridge back to app via Groupon Bucks
  • Target: ~30M US sessions/yr, $4-16M+ incremental
Together: Viktor fixes the bucket. We fill it with more water. Users we acquire through registration and extensions become the input for Viktor's personalisation system. Our off-platform data (what brands they shop, when they buy, what codes they use) feeds directly into the personalisation engine. Complementary layers of one ecosystem strategy.
Viktor's core finding

Clickstream Analysis: $166M marketing spend → +15% new users → only +1% revenue. 66% buy once and stop (7.3M users). Value ladder: $18 → $36 → $53 → $70 → $121. In-app: 62% never click a deal, 53% gone in 10s, 77% abandon checkout. "We're filling a bucket with a hole in the bottom."

Our strategy addresses this from the outside: rather than only fixing what happens when users are in the app, we extend Groupon's presence to where users already are - shopping on Safari. Every off-platform touchpoint creates a new reason to return to the app, where Viktor's personalisation can do its work.
