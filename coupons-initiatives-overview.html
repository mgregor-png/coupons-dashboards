Why cashback is a content + impressions lever

Today, coupons pages only have content for merchants that provide promo codes. Cashback lets us create an offer for any retailer - even those with zero existing content. This generates new impressions across web (coupons pages), Genie (desktop), and Safari (mobile).

The primary value is registration uplift (gating cashback behind account creation), SEO defense (more pages with real offers = stronger organic presence), and extension exposure (more merchants in the extension = more activation surface). Direct margin is modest after payout costs - the real return is in the funnel it feeds.

Testing approach: Build a cashback offer type, collect user emails, run a small number of manual delivery offers to validate the mechanics before scaling.

Three options

From quick wins on existing traffic, through extending what's already in progress, to bigger bets.

Option 1 - Easy wins
01 Monetise + 02 Register
On-page monetisation of non-monetised sessions (display ads, deal cross-sell). Registration gating with rewards unlocks identity layer at $3.83/reg LTV. Both work on existing 30M sessions with minimal engineering.

Option 2 - Genie + Rewards
03 Genie rewards + 03 Genie deals
Genie is already live with 981 installs and $6/user/yr commission. Rewards is the planned next iteration (Iteration 2) - gating higher-value rewards behind the extension drives installs and lifts per-user value from $6 to $7.74/yr (+29%). Adding Groupon deals cross-sell takes it to $9.81/yr. This isn't a new bet - it's executing the existing roadmap. Full LTV model ↗
Option 3 - Safari + Cashback
Safari ext + 04 Cashback
Safari extension inside the Groupon app reaches ~7-8M users (84% iOS), $6/user commission (small sample*). Requires iOS spike to validate, then full build. Cashback infrastructure (40-50 MD, most complex) creates content for any merchant - feeding impressions to web, Genie, and Safari. Drives registration uplift and SEO defense. These are the bigger bets with the highest ceiling.
* $6/user based on 981 installs, 388 transactions - directional, not statistically robust
