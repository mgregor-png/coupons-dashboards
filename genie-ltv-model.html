The Investment Question

736 users retained through 6 months of an incomplete product with no core feature (auto-apply). That validates demand. The retention gap between current (1.79x) and industry benchmark (2.0–2.4x) is the value that shipping auto-apply + rewards unlocks.

Decision gate: If retention improves to >2.0x within 90 days of auto-apply launch, continue investment. If retention stays below 1.9x after feature completion, reassess.

Benchmark: Honey (pre-acquisition) reported ~60% 12-month retention → ~2.5x multiplier. Auto-apply creates a "set and forget" habit loop that dramatically improves retention vs. active-use extensions.

Peak: 2,026 (Sep) → Trough: 736 (Feb) = 64% drop in 6 months. Mar rebound driven by popup launch. No auto-apply or rewards during this period.

Why the drop matters — and why it's misleading: Genie had no coupon auto-apply (the core value prop), no rewards, and minimal development. Users installed but found limited ongoing value. This is feature-incompleteness churn, not product-market-fit failure. 736 users staying active with no core feature is a demand signal.
